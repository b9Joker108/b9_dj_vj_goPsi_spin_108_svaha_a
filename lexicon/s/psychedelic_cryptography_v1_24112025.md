# Psychedelic Cryptography #

Define: 'Psychedelic Cryptography' with triangulated integrity and archival depth

Psychedelic cryptography is a novel, interdisciplinary concept combining principles of cryptography with the unique perceptual and cognitive effects induced by psychedelic substances. It refers to **encoding information into sensory stimuli such that the encrypted messages become fully accessible or decodable primarily when observed under altered states of consciousness elicited by psychedelics** [2][3].

### Triangulated Definition and Archival Integrity

- **Cryptography Context:** Cryptography is the scientific and mathematical practice of encoding information to secure it, typically involving encryption algorithms and keys such that the message is only recoverable by authorized parties [1][5].
- **Psychedelic Modulation:** Psychedelic states produce characteristic changes in perception—such as visual tracers, hyperbolic geometry, and enhanced pattern recognition—that create a phenomenological window where certain encoded sensory inputs can be uniquely or more efficiently deciphered [2][4].
- **Psychedelic Cryptography (PsyCrypto):** Formally defined by researcher Andrés Gómez-Emilsson in 2015, PsyCrypto proposes **encoding schemes that exploit psychedelic brain dynamics and perceptual alterations, turning psychedelic states into a computational resource for information processing and hidden communications**. This transcends classical cryptography by utilizing an "information processing advantage" conferred by altered consciousness [2][3][6].
- **Applications and Implications:** PsyCrypto has been applied in experimental video art with hidden messages visible primarily during psychedelic trips, serving as proof of concept of enhanced cognitive decoding. This opens new interdisciplinary research avenues in neuroscience, consciousness studies, cryptography, and experiential aesthetics [2][3][8].

### Archival and Ontological Depth

- At its core, Psychedelic Cryptography situates itself at the intersection of **phenomenological ontology** (study of experience), **information theory**, and **cryptographic security theory**.
- It challenges classical views of information encoding by positing **consciousness as an active computational phase space** modulated by neurochemical states, enabling forms of secure communication and knowledge transmission not accessible in baseline states [2][4].
- This groundwork has implications for understanding consciousness as a computational resource, with PsyCrypto encoding tapping into "phenomenological facts" (e.g., spatial-temporal frequency enhancements on DMT) for cryptographic advantage [2][4].
- PsyCrypto thus furnishes a **deep synthesis between experiential phenomenology and cryptographic practice, preserved in both digital archives (experimental videos, encoded datasets) and textual research in consciousness and security sciences** [2][3][8].
